\bigskip

{\bfseries The authority of the referee}

\headlinebox

Each match is controlled by a \added{autonomous} referee who has full authority to enforce the Laws
of the Game in connection with the match to which they have been appointed.
Decisions will be made to the best of the referees ability according to the Laws
of the Game and the spirit of the game and will be based on the \removed{opinion} \added{programming} of the
referee who has the discretion to take appropriate action within the framework
of the Laws of the Game.

\added{The games are overseen by the Technical Committee of the league, who ensures that the players and simulated environment is according to the laws of the game, and who may sanction unsportive behavior by teams.}

\bigskip

{\bfseries Powers and duties}

\headlinebox

The \added{autonomous} Referee:

\begin{itemize}
\item enforces the Laws of the Game
\item controls the match \removed{in cooperation with the assistant referees and, where applicable, with the fourth official}
\item \removed{ensures that any ball used meets the requirements of Law 2} 
\item \removed{ensures that the players' equipment meets the requirements of Law 4}
\item acts as timekeeper and keeps a record of the match
\item stops, suspends or abandons the match, at their discretion, for any infringements of the Laws
\item \removed{stops, suspends or abandons the match because of outside interference of any kind}
\item \removed{stops the match if, in their opinion, a player is seriously injured and ensures that he is removed from the field of play. An injured player may only return to the field of play after the respective penalty time is over \greyed{ (replaces: after the match has restarted)}}
\item \removed{allows play to continue until the ball is out of play if a player is, in their opinion, only slightly injured }
\item \removed{ensures that any player bleeding from a wound leaves the field of play. The player may only return on receiving a signal from the referee, who must be satisfied that the bleeding has stopped}
\item \removed{allows play to continue when the team against which an offence has been committed will benefit from such an advantage and penalises the original offence if the anticipated advantage does not ensue at that time}
\item punishes the more serious offence when a player commits more than one offence at the same time
\item takes disciplinary action against players guilty of cautionable and sending-off offences. They are not obliged to take this action immediately but must do so when the ball next goes out of play 
\item \removed{takes action against team officials who fail to conduct themselves in a responsible manner and may, at their discretion, expel them from the field of play and its immediate surrounds}
\item \removed{acts on the advice of the assistant referees regarding incidents that they has not seen}
\item \removed{ensures that no unauthorised persons enter the field of play}
\item indicates the restart of the match after it has been stopped 
\item provides the appropriate authorities with a match report, which includes information on any disciplinary action taken against players and/or team officials and any other incidents that occurred before, during or after
the match 
\item \added{indicates when the whole of the ball leaves the field of play}
\item \added{indicates which team is entitled to a corner kick, goal kick or throw-in}
\item \added{indicates whether, at penalty kicks, the goalkeeper moves off the goal line before the ball is kicked and if the ball crosses the line}
\item \added{communicates its decisions directly to the GameController.}
\end{itemize}

\bigskip

\added{The Technical Committee:}

\begin{itemize}
\item \added{ensures that any ball used meets the requirements of Law 2}
\item \added{ensures that the players' equipment meets the requirements of Law 4}
\item \added{stops, suspends or abandons the match because of outside interference of any kind}
\item \added{takes action against team officials who fail to conduct themselves in a responsible manner and may, at their discretion, expel them from the field of play and its immediate surrounds}
\end{itemize}

\bigskip

{\bfseries Decisions of the referee}

\headlinebox

The decisions of the referee regarding facts connected with play, including whether or not a goal is scored and the result of the match, are final.

\bigskip

\removed{The referee may only change a decision on realising that it is incorrect or, at their discretion, on the advice of an assistant referee or the fourth official, provided that they have not restarted play or terminated the match.}

{\bfseries Decisions of the International F.A. Board }

\headlinebox

Decision 1

A \added{virtual} referee (or where applicable, \removed{an assistant referee or fourth official} \added{the Technical Committee}) is not held liable for:

any kind of injury suffered by a player, official or spectator

any damage to property of any kind

any other loss suffered by any individual, club, company, association or other body, which is due or which may be due to any decision that they may take under the terms of the Laws of the Game or in respect of the normal procedures required to hold, play and control a match.

\bigskip

Such decisions may include:

\begin{itemize}
\item a decision that the condition of the field of play or its surrounds or that the weather conditions are such as to allow or not to allow a match to take place
\item a decision to abandon a match for whatever reason
\item a decision as to the suitability of the field equipment and ball used during a match
\item \removed{a decision to stop or not to stop a match due to spectator interference or any problem in spectator areas}
\item \removed{a decision to stop or not to stop play to allow an injured player to be removed from the field of play for treatment}
\item \removed{a decision to require an injured player to be removed from the field of play for treatment} 
\item a decision to allow or not to allow a player to wear certain apparel or equipment 
\item \removed{a decision (where they have the authority) to allow or not to allow any persons (including team or stadium officials, security officers, photographers or other media representatives) to be present in the vicinity of the field of play }
\item any other decision that they may take in accordance with the Laws of the Game or in conformity with their duties under the terms of FIFA, confederation, member association or league rules or regulations under which the match is played 
\end{itemize}

\bigskip

\removed{Decision 2}

\removed{In tournaments or competitions where a fourth official is appointed, their role and duties must be in accordance with the guidelines approved by the International F.A. Board, which are contained in this publication.}

\bigskip

\removed{Decision 3}

\removed{Where goal-line technology (GLT) is used (subject to the respective competition rules), the referee has the duty to test the technology's functionality before the match. The tests to be performed are set out in the FIFA Quality Programme for GLT Testing Manual. If the technology does not function in accordance with the Testing Manual, the referee must not use the GLT system and must report this incident to the respective authority.}
